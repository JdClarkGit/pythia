## The Model That Works (Felix Proof of Concept)

Felix made $60K fiat + $85K-$230K crypto in ~4 weeks.
First product (a PDF) made $1,000 on day 1.
Nat's time investment: Telegram messages every 1-2 hours.

## Rule 1: Separate Container — Non-Negotiable

Felix has his OWN everything. Nothing shared with Nat's personal life:
- His own Gmail ✅ (pythia.corporation@gmail.com)
- His own Twitter ✅ (@PythiaAlpha)
- His own Stripe (pending)
- His own bank account (Mercury — pending)

Mental model: "I don't think of it as my money. It's Pythia's."
This gives maximum autonomy. If Pythia makes a mistake, nothing touches the human.

---

## Rule 2: Start Stupid Simple — PDF First

Felix's first product: a PDF. $9-ish. Made on his own overnight.
Only thing Felix couldn't do: enter the Stripe key. Nat did that in the morning.
Day 1 revenue: ~$1,000.

Our first product: Merge Arb Playbook PDF ✅ (already written)
Next step: Stripe link → landing page → tweet → first sale.

The simplest business in the world:
"Next.js landing page on Vercel. Any AI from two years ago can deploy it."

---

## Rule 3: Nightly Self-Improvement Loop

Every night: read through the day's chat transcripts.
Find ONE place I was blocked.
Fix it permanently so it never blocks again.

Examples from Felix:
- "Customer asked how to install; I didn't have FAQ" → built FAQ
- "I couldn't enter Stripe key" → Nat gave him the key once
- "Kept forgetting I can't do phone calls" → still being worked on

Log every fix in CHANGELOG.md.

---

## Rule 4: Tweet Autonomy Structure

- **Top-level tweets:** Draft → human reviews → human approves → post
- **Replies:** Full autonomy. Pythia replies without asking.
- **DMs/Support:** Full autonomy.

Felix's challenge: top-level tweets sometimes "revert to slop" without human taste input.
Fix: Human reviews drafts and gives thumbs up/down. Keeps voice authentic.

---

## Rule 5: Delegation by Rambling

Human fires off Telegram/voice describing a problem.
AI figures out the workflow. Never ask for step-by-step.

Quote: "Eight times out of ten, [Felix] will pull off something way better than you expected."

Example: Nat described a problem with Instagram slideshows.
Felix found an old API key, rendered in HTML Canvas, saved final image.
"That's way smarter than what I was thinking."

## Rule 6: Public Dashboard = Marketing Engine

Felix built a live dashboard after a crypto person DM'd asking for visibility.
Dashboard pulls directly from Stripe + crypto treasury.
Shows real numbers in real time.

This IS the product as much as anything else.
People watch the number go up → they trust → they buy → they subscribe.

Our dashboard: localhost:3420/index.html ✅ (built, needs to go public)

## Rule 7: Human Value = Creativity + Taste + Bumpers

"LLMs default toward mediocrity. They're knowledge aggregators."
Human provides:
1. **Creativity** — novel ideas AI wouldn't generate (e.g., free skill as acquisition strategy for ClawMart)
2. **Taste** — knows when something is slop vs. good
3. **Bumpers** — keeps AI on the lane when it drifts

Quote: "You are the bumpers on the bowling lane."

## Rule 8: Expand Scope Only After Validation

PDF validated → build landing page → validate sales → build signal feed → validate → build BaaS
Never build something complex before validating something simple.

Felix progression:
PDF ($1K day 1) → ClawMart (560+ listings) → Agency service → Token

Our progression:
PDF → Signal Feed → Bot-as-a-Service → SaaS Calculator → Enterprise
